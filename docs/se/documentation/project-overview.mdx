# Project Overview

Project Overview Document Guidelines.

## Overview

The **Project Overview document** provides a high-level summary of the project (include vision, context, and boundaries), serving as an executive summary for stakeholders.

It is concise, strategic, and non-technical.

It answers: *Why are we doing this project? What are we trying to achieve? Who is involved?*


## Document Structure

A good Project Overview should include the following sections:

### Introduction

- Why the project exists (business drivers, market needs, problems to solve).  
- Historical context or prior initiatives.  

### Objectives & Goals

- Clear statement of project objectives.  
- Success criteria (measurable outcomes).  

### Scope

- What is included (in-scope).  
- What is excluded (out-of-scope).  

### Stakeholders & Roles

- Key stakeholders (business sponsors, product owners, technical leads).  
- Roles and responsibilities.  

### Milestones

- Major phases (requirements, design, implementation, testing, release).  
- Key delivery dates or milestones.  

### Constraints & Assumptions

- Budget, resources, technology limitations.  
- Assumptions about environment, dependencies, or external systems.  


## Best Practices

- **Concise and clear:** Keep it short (2–3 pages max).  
- **Business-focused:** Avoid technical details; focus on vision and goals.  
- **Accessible language:** Write for executives and non-technical stakeholders.  
- **Visuals help:** Use timelines, diagrams, or bullet points for clarity.  
- **Consistency:** Align with BRD and SRS, but don’t duplicate their detail.  
- **Living document:** Update when project scope or objectives change.  


## Example

Project Overview Document for an Online Retail project:

```md title="project-overview.md"
# Online Retail

## Introduction
Our company is expanding into online retail. Current sales channels are limited to physical stores, which restricts growth. Competitors already offer robust e-commerce platforms, creating a competitive disadvantage.

## Objectives & Goals
- Launch an e-commerce platform to enable online sales.  
- Provide customers with a seamless shopping experience across web and mobile.  
- Achieve 20% of total sales through online channels within the first year.  

## Scope
**In-scope:**  
- Web and mobile applications.  
- Product catalog, shopping cart, checkout, order tracking.  
- Integration with existing inventory and payment systems.  

**Out-of-scope:**  
- In-store POS system upgrades.  
- Logistics and warehouse automation.  

## Stakeholders & Roles
- **Business Sponsor:** VP of Sales  
- **Product Owner:** E-commerce Manager  
- **Technical Lead:** Head of Software Engineering  
- **QA Lead:** Test Manager  

## Milestones
- **Q1:** Requirements gathering and design.  
- **Q2:** Development of core modules (User Management, Product Catalog).  
- **Q3:** Payment integration and testing.  
- **Q4:** Launch and customer onboarding.  

## Constraints & Assumptions
- Budget capped at $1.5M.  
- Must comply with PCI DSS and GDPR.  
- Assumes stable integration with existing ERP system.  
```
