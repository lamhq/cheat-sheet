# Chain of Thought Prompting

## Introduction

Large language models struggle with **multi-step reasoning tasks** like math word problems and common sense reasoning—tasks that are easy for humans but difficult for models.

**Chain of Thought** is a prompting technique that breaks down complex problems into **intermediate reasoning steps**, allowing models to solve problems they otherwise couldn't.

**Key idea:** Guide the model to solve problems the way humans do—step by step.

## Example

```md
**Example**:
Q: Sean has 5 toys. He gets 2 from mom and 2 from dad. Total?
A: Sean starts with 5. Gets 2 from mom = 7. Gets 2 from dad = 9.
Answer: 9

**Now solve**:
Q: John takes care of 10 dogs. Each needs 5 hours/day. 
How many hours/week?
```

**Result:** Model learns the reasoning pattern and applies it correctly:
- 10 dogs × 5 hours/day = 50 hours/day
- 50 hours/day × 7 days = **350 hours/week**

## Why Chain of Thought Matters

- Models approach problems like humans do
- Complex reasoning becomes manageable
- More accurate answers on multi-step problems
- Model shows its work (transparency)
- Enables solving previously unsolvable problems
