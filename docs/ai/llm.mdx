# Large Language Models

## Introduction

Language modeling is essentially **predicting the next word in a sequence**. 

Think of it as a super-smart autocomplete feature. Given a sequence of words (like "the dog wagged its..."), a language model calculates the probability for each potential next word and outputs the one with the highest probability.

Formally, it computes the probability of the next word given all previous words in a sentence, where the next word must come from the model's vocabulary.

## Large Language Models (LLMs)

An LLM is simply a language model trained on **massive amounts of data**. This extensive training makes it exceptionally good at calculating those word probabilities accurately.

### How LLMs Work

When you give an LLM a prompt:
1. It takes your input words
2. Calculates the probability distribution for the next word
3. Outputs the word with the highest probability
4. Repeats this process word-by-word to generate a complete response

## Hallucinations

LLMs sometimes generate outputs that are completely false or disconnected from reality (hallucinations) because they fundamentally guess based on probability, not reasoning about truth.
